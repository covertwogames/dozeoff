Are your notifications arriving late — or not at all? You're not alone!

Android uses a system called Doze to save battery by putting apps to sleep. Unfortunately, Doze is often far too aggressive. Important notifications from messaging apps, email, smart home devices, security cameras, and more can be delayed by minutes or even hours.

DozeOff fixes this by sending periodic background pulses that keep your device alert and your notifications flowing on time — with minimal battery impact (typically 1-2% per day).

TWO LEVELS OF PROTECTION
=========================

Standard Mode sends regular background pulses that work within Android's normal battery rules. Most notification delays are significantly reduced with no visible indicators or side effects.

Max Mode uses a more powerful method that Android cannot delay or skip. Every pulse fires exactly on schedule, flushing all pending notifications, messages, and syncs. Significantly more effective than standard mode for users who need reliable, real-time alerts.
